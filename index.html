Adaptive Digital Journaling

A local-storage journaling prototype for everyday study-life reflection

Prototype notice: This is not monitored live and does not provide emergency or mental health support. Please avoid real names, addresses, phone numbers, or identifiable details.

Welcome

This prototype helps students choose a journaling mode based on their current study-life state. It explores whether adaptive prompts can match students’ daily states, support their journaling needs, and guide everyday self-reflection in a more situated way.

Quick Reflection

A short, low-burden mode for high-pressure or low-energy days.

Brief Reflection

A medium-depth mode for connecting an event with feelings or study state.

Guided Reflection

A deeper mode for complex emotions, meaningful events, and future intentions.

How it works

  1. Complete a short daily check-in.
  2. Receive a suggested journaling mode.
  3. Accept or switch the mode.
  4. Write a journal entry using separate prompt boxes.
  5. Review previous entries, hide entries if needed, and add later reflection.

Participant setup

Research metadata

When you save an entry, a later reflection, or hide/unhide an entry, the research metadata for that action is sent to the researcher’s secure Google Sheet. This includes your participant ID, check-in ratings, mood label, selected mode, entry length, feedback ratings, your written feedback about the experience, the pattern you selected when revisiting an entry, the length of your later-reflection notes, and hide/unhide actions.

Your private writing is never uploaded: your answers to the journaling prompts, the optional extra note, and the text of your later reflections (your new thoughts and your next small step) all stay only in this browser. Only their word count is shared.

Offline backup (optional)

You can also download your own copy of the same metadata as CSV files. This is a backup and is only needed if asked by the researcher.
